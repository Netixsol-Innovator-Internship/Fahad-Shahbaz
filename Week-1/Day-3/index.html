Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about traveling to Saudi Arabia

Yes, most visitors need a visa to enter Saudi Arabia. However, Saudi Arabia offers tourist visas for citizens of eligible countries. You can apply for an eVisa online or get a visa on arrival at major airports. The tourist visa allows multiple entries and is valid for one year.

The best time to visit Saudi Arabia is during the cooler months from October to March. During this period, temperatures are more comfortable for sightseeing and outdoor activities. The summer months (April to September) can be extremely hot, especially in inland areas.

Saudi Arabia has relaxed its dress code for tourists. While modest clothing is still recommended, the strict abaya requirement for women has been lifted for tourists. Both men and women should wear clothing that covers shoulders and knees. Lightweight, breathable fabrics are recommended due to the climate.

Yes, Saudi Arabia is considered very safe for tourists. The country has low crime rates and has invested heavily in tourism infrastructure and security. The government has implemented various measures to ensure tourist safety and comfort throughout the kingdom.

The official currency of Saudi Arabia is the Saudi Riyal (SAR). Credit cards are widely accepted in major cities and tourist areas. ATMs are readily available, and most international cards work. It's advisable to carry some cash for smaller establishments and traditional markets.

While the holy cities of Mecca and Medina are restricted to Muslims only, non-Muslims can visit many other historical and cultural sites throughout Saudi Arabia, including ancient ruins, museums, and cultural centers that showcase the country's rich Islamic heritage and history.
